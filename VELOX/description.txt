The thermal-infrared imager VELOX (Video airbornE Longwave Observations within siX channels) onboard HALO provided 2D brightness temperature (BT) fields of the surface and cloud tops during the PERCUSION campaign. VELOX has a field-of-view of 35.5° x 28.7° with 640 x 512 spatial pixels. For a target distance of 10 km the spatial resolution is about 10 m. The imager has two redundant broadband and four narrow-band channels in the wavelength range from 7.7 µm to 12.0 µm.

The dataset comprises brightness temperatures measured in one of the broadband channels (BT1: 7.7 µm - 12 µm) and four narrow-band channels with respective central wavelengths and half-widths at 8.65 +/- 0.55 µm (BT2), 10.74 +/- 0.39 µm (BT3), 11.66 +/- 0.81 µm (BT5), and 12.00 +/- 0.50 µm (BT6). The brightness temperature measurements were corrected for bad pixels and effects introduced by the housing window. A destriping procedure was applied for the spectral channels only. To allow cross-channel comparability the image size was adapted according to a channel footprint alignment. Therefore, 2D brightness temperature fields with a size of 635 x 507 pixels and a temporal resolution of 1 Hz are provided. In addition, cloud-free simulations are provided for all channels with a temporal resolution of 1 min.

No cross-calibration is available. Therefore, we recommend using the given dataset with care and to rely on relative values rather than absolute values. Nevertheless, the cloud-free simulations can be used as a guideline to identify possible offsets in the measured brightness temperature. We recommend using the mean of the 10 x 10 central pixels (variable “BT_center”) for comparison to the simulations.
